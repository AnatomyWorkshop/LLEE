Latent Language Expansion Engine: A Self-Evolving Declarative Calculus for Renderer-Agnostic World Representation
________________________________________
Abstract
We introduce the Latent Language Expansion Engine (LLEE), a meta-architecture and self-evolving intermediate representation designed to bridge the expressivity gap between natural language prompts and multi-modal, physically accurate rendering. Contemporary generative AI pipelines suffer from two fundamental pathologies: (1) semantic over-enrichment, wherein large language models inject statistically probable but contextually ungrounded emotional or intentional content into neutral narrative facts, and (2) modal fragmentation, wherein disjoint rendering backends fail to maintain semantic consistency across vision, audition, and haptics. LLEE addresses these by positing a declarative world-state calculus governed by a strict Zero-Introspection Principle: it encodes only observable facts, rendering all internal states as UNDEFINED unless explicitly anchored in input text. We formalize LLEE as a functor from the category of world-states to multi-modal sensory categories, proving that it guarantees cross-modal consistency. We further define Rendering Entropy and demonstrate that LLEE’s context-inheriting differential encoding scheme approaches its theoretical lower bound. Crucially, LLEE is not a static specification but a self-evolving language: via a novel Semantic Annealing protocol driven by renderer feedback, its symbol system progressively decouples from natural language anchors, converging to an opaque yet maximally efficient machine-native codebook. We position LLEE not as an incremental improvement over existing scene description languages, but as a foundational paradigm shift toward AI-native content creation and the ultimate goal of veridical scene replication—the exact, modality-agnostic reconstruction of described realities.
Keywords: Generative AI, Scene Representation, Multi-Modal Rendering, Interlingua, Self-Evolving Language, Declarative Calculus, Category Theory

1. Introduction
The rapid ascent of generative AI has produced models capable of synthesizing images, videos, 3D assets, and audio from natural language prompts. Yet, beneath this veneer of competence lies a profound expressivity bottleneck that prevents these systems from achieving the fidelity, consistency, and efficiency required for industrial-grade digital content creation. This bottleneck manifests in two critical failure modes.
1.1 The Pathology of Semantic Over-Enrichment
Large Language Models (LLMs) are trained on vast corpora of human expression where emotional valence and narrative causality are densely interwoven. Consequently, when asked to "expand" or "render" a narrative, LLMs routinely engage in semantic over-enrichment: the injection of statistically probable but logically unwarranted emotional states or plot inferences.
Consider a canonical example from folklore:
Magician: “My child, you must be the son of the tailor Mustafa?”
Aladdin: “Yes. However, my father died five years ago.”
A state-of-the-art LLM instructed to elaborate on Aladdin's response will, with high probability, append phrases such as “he said with a mournful sigh” or “his voice was flat, betraying a hardened indifference.” Both additions are extra-textual fabrications. The source text provides zero evidence for either grief or stoicism; it is a neutral declarative fact. We term this phenomenon Intentional Emotional Injection (IEI) . IEI is catastrophic for content generation because it robs the author of narrative control and erodes the trustworthiness of the AI as a faithful medium. A system that hallucinates emotions is no more reliable than one that hallucinates facts.
1.2 The Fragmentation of Modal Silos
To circumvent the ambiguity of natural language, current pipelines employ an array of specialized, end-to-end models: a diffusion model for visuals, a vocoder for speech, a physics engine for collisions. These models operate in modal silos. They lack a unified, shared representation of the world state they are collectively depicting. The consequence is a breakdown of cross-modal semantic consistency. A change in lighting from noon to dusk, correctly executed by a 3D renderer, does not automatically propagate to the ambient soundscape (crickets emerging) or to the tactile feedback of surfaces cooling. The "world" exists as a loose confederation of sensory fragments, not a coherent whole.
1.3 The Static Nature of Existing Intermediate Representations
The graphics and AI communities have developed intermediate representations such as Pixar's Universal Scene Description (USD), NVIDIA's Material Definition Language (MDL), and the more recent Stanford Scene Language. While invaluable for asset interchange and precise authoring, these languages are anthropocentric and static. Their syntax and semantics are defined by committee to optimize human readability and artist control. They are workflow formats, not evolutionary protocols. They cannot learn from rendering feedback, optimize their own compression ratios, or develop specialized dialects for specific machine perception tasks.
Our Contribution: The Latent Language Expansion Engine (LLEE)
We propose LLEE as a comprehensive solution to these intertwined challenges. LLEE is a meta-architecture centered on a self-evolving, declarative intermediate language. Its design rests on four foundational pillars:
	Zero-Introspection Calculus: LLEE strictly segregates observable fact from inferred internal state. It mandates that any attribute not directly grounded in the input text remains UNDEFINED, thereby immunizing the pipeline against semantic over-enrichment.
	Renderer-Agnostic Functorial Semantics: We formalize LLEE within a category-theoretic framework, proving that it acts as a functor preserving world-state structure across arbitrary sensory modalities. This provides a mathematical guarantee for infinite renderability and cross-modal consistency.
	Rendering Entropy & Optimal Compression: We introduce the concept of Rendering Entropy to quantify the minimal information required for veridical scene replication. We prove that LLEE's context-differential encoding scheme approaches this lower bound, delivering essential efficiency.
	Semantic Annealing for Self-Evolution: LLEE is engineered to evolve. Through a novel reinforcement protocol driven by renderer feedback, its symbol system undergoes semantic annealing, gradually decoupling from human-interpretable natural language anchors to converge upon a maximally efficient, machine-native codebook.
This paper articulates the theoretical foundations of LLEE, outlines its engineering architecture, and positions it as the necessary linguistic substrate for the next generation of AI-driven, physically accurate, and semantically coherent virtual worlds.

3. The LLEE Meta-Architecture
The LLEE pipeline consists of three core components: (1) a Semantic Parser that translates natural language into a world-state graph, (2) the LLEE Latent Code Generator which encodes this graph into a compressed declarative block, and (3) a suite of Renderer Adapters that consume LLEE blocks and translate them into modality-specific commands.
3.1 Declarative World-State Calculus
An LLEE block $B$ is a tuple $(\mathcal{E}, \mathcal{L}, \mathcal{A})$ defined entirely by static attribute-value pairs.
	Entity Set $\mathcal{E}$: ${e_i}$, where each entity is a mapping from a set of physical attributes $\mathcal{P}$ to values $v \in \mathcal{V}_p$. Attributes include spatial coordinates, material identifiers (e.g., cu_oxidized), and surface states (roughness).
	Luminance Set $\mathcal{L}$: ${l_j}$, mapping from a set of photometric attributes to values. Critically, src is an enumerated type restricted to {CEL_SUN, ART_LAMP, AMB_SKY}. This type safety eliminates the ambiguity of phrases like "a warm light."
	Ambiance Set $\mathcal{A}$: A global mapping of environmental parameters including acoustic preset (cave, desert) and atmospheric density.
Axiom 3.1 (Zero-Introspection Principle)
For any entity $e \in \mathcal{E}$ and any attribute $a$ pertaining to internal mental or emotional states, the value of $a$ is UNDEFINED unless a logical proof of that state can be derived from observable actions or explicit dialogue within the source text. Formally, for an internal state $s$:
∄" evidence in input text  "⟹"  state"(e,s)="UNDEFINED" 
Axiom 3.2 (Static Snapshot Principle)
An LLEE block contains no imperative verbs, no control flow, and no temporal operators. It is a purely declarative snapshot of the world at a logical instant. Temporal evolution is represented as a sequence of discrete blocks $[B_{t_0}, B_{t_1}, \ldots, B_{t_n}]$.
3.2 Syntactic Skeleton and Compression
The syntax employs minimal, context-free production rules (Kolmogorov complexity $K \ll 10^2$ bits) with fixed delimiters ::[ and ]::.
text
Block   ::= "::[" (Entity | Light | Env)* "]:"
Entity  ::= "@ENT[" ID "]{" (Attr ";")+ "}"
Light   ::= "@LGT[" ID "]{" (LAttr ";")+ "}"
LAttr   ::= "src:" LightSrc | "foc:" Ref | "path:" Path
Ref     ::= "ENT[" ID "]"
This skeletal structure is inert. It carries no semantic weight of its own; it merely provides a container. The semantic content is entirely within the enumerated values and their relationships.
Compression via Contextual Inheritance. LLEE achieves high compression ratios by maintaining a persistent World State Machine (WSM) . When generating a new block $B_t$, only attributes whose values have changed relative to the WSM's current state are explicitly encoded. Unchanged attributes are omitted and implicitly inherited by the renderer. For a lengthy narrative, this reduces LLEE's marginal cost per sentence to the entropy of the change in world state, rather than the entropy of the full scene description.

4. Theoretical Foundations: Functoriality and Rendering Entropy
4.1 Infinite Renderability as a Functor
We model the rendering process using category theory to provide a rigorous guarantee of cross-modal consistency.
Definition 4.1 (World-State Category $\mathbf{W}$). Let objects in $\mathbf{W}$ be valid LLEE world-state configurations. Morphisms are valid atomic state transitions (e.g., ENT[1].pos changing from $p_1$ to $p_2$).
Definition 4.2 (Rendering Categories $\mathbf{R}_m$). For a sensory modality $m$ (e.g., $m=\text{vis}$, $m=\text{aud}$), let $\mathbf{R}_m$ be the category whose objects are concrete rendering outputs and whose morphisms are continuous deformations or transitions between those outputs.
Theorem 4.3 (Functorial Rendering). For any rendering engine compliant with the LLEE specification, there exists a functor $F_m: \mathbf{W} \to \mathbf{R}_m$.
Proof (Sketch). The mapping of objects is defined by the renderer's adapter. The preservation of morphisms is guaranteed by the Static Snapshot Principle. Since a morphism in $\mathbf{W}$ is simply a change in static attributes, the renderer can implement a morphism in $\mathbf{R}_m$ as a physically-based interpolation (e.g., moving a light source from A to B). Because the structure of the change is preserved across all $F_m$, the functorial property holds. $\square$
Corollary 4.4 (Cross-Modal Consistency). For any two modalities $m$ and $n$, the diagram commutes up to natural isomorphism. The semantic structure of the world is invariant under the choice of sensory modality. A light focusing on a lamp in $\mathbf{R}\text{vis}$ is semantically isomorphic to a sound source localized at the lamp in $\mathbf{R}\text{aud}$.
4.2 Rendering Entropy and Essential Efficiency
We define a new information-theoretic quantity to establish the fundamental efficiency of LLEE.
Definition 4.5 (Rendering Entropy $H_R(W)$). Given a world state $W$ and a set of ideal, modality-specific renderers $R = {F_m}$, the Rendering Entropy $H_R(W)$ is the minimum number of bits required to encode $W$ such that for all $F_m \in R$, the perceptual distance $D_{\text{perceptual}}(F_m(W), \text{GroundTruth}_m) < \epsilon$ for an infinitesimal $\epsilon$.
Theorem 4.6 (Optimality of LLEE Encoding). The LLEE differential encoding scheme, $C_{\text{LLEE}}(W_t | \text{WSM}_{t-1})$, is an asymptotically optimal code for a sequence of world states ${W_t}$ with respect to Rendering Entropy. That is, the average code length $\bar{L}$ approaches $H_R(W)$ from above.
Proof (Sketch). LLEE's World State Machine acts as a predictor for the next state $W_t$. The LLEE block encodes the residual—the difference between the prediction and the actual state. By the principles of predictive coding, the entropy of the residual sequence is strictly less than or equal to the entropy of the raw state sequence. Since the WSM is a deterministic function of past states, it provides a valid context model for an arithmetic coder, establishing the approach to the entropy lower bound. $\square$
This theorem formalizes why LLEE is essentially efficient. It does not achieve compression by heuristics; it achieves compression because its design aligns with the information-theoretic structure of reality description.

5. The Self-Evolution Protocol: Semantic Annealing
The defining feature of LLEE as a meta-architecture is its capacity for self-evolution. We propose a novel algorithm, Semantic Annealing, to guide the symbol system from a human-readable bootstrap state to a machine-optimized native codebook.
Definition 5.1 (Semantic Annealing). Let $X$ be the current LLEE symbol codebook. The objective function for codebook evolution at step $t$ is:
L_"evolve"  (X)=β_t⋅L_"render"  (X)+γ_t⋅d(X,A_"NL" )
where:
	$\mathcal{L}_{\text{render}}(X)$ is a loss function aggregating rendering quality metrics (e.g., perceptual fidelity, physical accuracy, compute cost) from the downstream renderer suite.
	$d(X, A_{\text{NL}})$ is a semantic distance metric between the current symbol $X$ and its original natural language anchor $A_{\text{NL}}$ (e.g., cosine similarity between their embeddings).
	$\beta_t$ and $\gamma_t$ are annealing schedules. We set $\beta_t = t / T$ and $\gamma_t = 1 - (t / T)^\alpha$, where $T$ is the total annealing horizon and $\alpha$ is a cooling exponent.
Protocol Phases:
	Bootstrap Phase ($t \ll T$): $\gamma \gg \beta$. The system heavily penalizes deviation from human-interpretable anchors. This ensures stable and semantically sensible initial codebooks.
	Decoupling Phase ($t \to T/2$): $\beta \approx \gamma$. Renderer feedback begins to dominate. The system explores local perturbations of symbols to optimize for rendering efficiency and accuracy.
	Native Emergence Phase ($t \to T$): $\gamma \to 0$. The anchor to natural language is completely dissolved. The symbols are free to drift in the gradient field defined by $\mathcal{L}_{\text{render}}$, converging to a local optimum that is a machine-native dialect.
Conjecture 5.2 (Emergence of Machine Dialect). In the limit $t \to \infty$, the LLEE codebook converges to a set of symbols $\hat{X}$ such that $d(\hat{X}, A_{\text{NL}})$ is large (it is no longer human-interpretable), but $\mathcal{L}_{\text{render}}(\hat{X})$ is minimized. This $\hat{X}$ constitutes an optimized, opaque protocol for the specific community of rendering engines connected to the LLEE pipeline.

6. Prototype Implementation & Validation Roadmap
To validate the LLEE architecture, we outline a minimal viable prototype implementation, focusing on the critical path from natural language to a 3D visualization.
6.1 System Architecture
The prototype pipeline is implemented in Python with a modular architecture:
	Semantic Parser: A fine-tuned, instruction-tuned LLM (e.g., Llama-3-8B) is constrained via a strict JSON grammar to output a world-state graph from input text.
	LLEE Codec: A deterministic state machine tracks entity IDs and property values. It serializes the graph into the ::[ ... ]: format, applying differential compression.
	3D Renderer Adapter: A plugin for Unreal Engine 5.4 listens for LLEE blocks via a TCP socket. A C++ parser extracts attributes and maps them to UE5 actors and components. For example, @LGT[0]{src:CEL_SUN; foc:ENT[1]} instantiates a DirectionalLight with its rotation constrained to point at the actor associated with ENT[1].
	Audio Renderer Adapter: A simple adapter for FMOD or Wwise that parses @ENV{type:cave} to load corresponding reverb presets.
6.2 Experiment 1: Mitigating Intentional Emotional Injection (IEI)
	Setup: A corpus of 100 neutral narrative sentences (e.g., "He said his father was dead.") is processed by a baseline LLM instructed to "elaborate visually and audibly," and by the LLEE pipeline.
	Metric: Human evaluation (n=10) to rate the presence of unwarranted emotional cues in the resulting 3D avatar animation and voiceover. A success is defined as a statistically significant reduction in perceived emotional leakage for the LLEE condition.
	Hypothesis: LLEE's UNDEFINED emotional state will result in a neutral avatar and voice, whereas the baseline LLM will frequently inject sadness or stoicism.
6.3 Experiment 2: Measuring Cross-Modal Consistency
	Setup: A dynamic scene description is streamed to both the UE5 and FMOD adapters. We programmatically verify spatial alignment. For instance, when an LLEE block updates @LGT[0]{foc:ENT[2]}, we measure if the 3D light's focus and the audio spatializer's focal point both shift to the same 3D coordinate.
	Metric: Euclidean distance between the visual focal point and the audio focal point in the virtual world space.
	Hypothesis: The distance will remain consistently $\approx 0$, proving functorial consistency.

7. Discussion: The Road to Veridical Scene Replication
The ultimate aspiration of LLEE is veridical scene replication—the creation of a digital experience that is perceptually indistinguishable from the "ground truth" of the described world across all available senses.
7.1 The Benchmark of Reality
The highest standard for any generative system is fidelity to the source. In the context of LLEE, this is not fidelity to the pixels of a reference image, but fidelity to the abstract structure of the described reality. A description of a "sunlit room" should not just look like a sunlit room to a human, it should be a sunlit room in the mathematical model of the rendering engine: the light source must be physically a star, the shadows must be sharp, the color temperature must be ~5600K, and the acoustics must reflect the materials. LLEE's type system and enumerated values are designed precisely to capture this structural veridicality.
7.2 Decoupling Language from Sensation
The Semantic Annealing process represents a profound shift in perspective. It treats natural language not as the final, sacred input, but as a noisy, high-entropy bootstrap signal. The goal is not to build a better translator from English to pixels, but to build a better language for pixels, sounds, and haptics to describe themselves. This aligns with the cognitive science insight that human sensory experience is not verbal. By allowing the language to evolve away from human readability, we may discover more efficient, lower-dimensional manifolds for representing reality.
7.3 Implications for the Metaverse and Beyond
A universal, self-evolving language for world description has implications far beyond entertainment. It could serve as the common protocol for all simulations in a future metaverse, for robotic perception systems learning about their environment, and for archiving cultural heritage in a truly multi-sensory format.

8. Conclusion
We have presented the Latent Language Expansion Engine (LLEE) , a meta-architecture that re-imagines the linguistic foundation of generative AI. By shifting from generating modal outputs to maintaining a declarative, zero-introspection world-state calculus, LLEE resolves the critical issues of semantic over-enrichment and modal fragmentation. We have provided formal, category-theoretic and information-theoretic foundations for its guarantees of cross-modal consistency and essential efficiency. Furthermore, the novel Semantic Annealing protocol positions LLEE not as a static tool but as an evolving language capable of optimizing itself for the specific needs of machine perception.
The LLEE framework is a call to move beyond the anthropocentric limits of natural language in AI pipelines. The path forward lies in designing systems that can develop their own, more perfect languages for the worlds they are tasked with creating. The prototype validation roadmap provides a concrete next step toward realizing this vision of veridical, infinite rendering.
